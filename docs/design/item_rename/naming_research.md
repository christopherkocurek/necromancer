# Naming Research - The Necromancer
## RESEARCH-001: Third Age Tolkien Naming Conventions

---

## Design Principles Reminder

1. **tolkien_grounded**: Names should evoke Middle-earth, not generic fantasy
2. **layer_appropriate**: Items found deeper should sound darker/more corrupted
3. **functional_clarity**: Names must still communicate what the item does
4. **no_first_age**: Avoid Silmarillion-era references. Third Age only.

## Regional Styles

### Gondor (Númenórean Heritage)
- **Style**: Noble, ancient, formal Sindarin/Quenya
- **Materials**: Steel, silver, black iron
- **Examples**: Andúril (Flame of the West), Narsil
- **Armor**: Chain mail, helms like Crown of Gondor, vambraces, greaves
- **Naming pattern**: [Quality/Material] + [Weapon type]
- **Appropriate for**: Noble weapons, ancestral items, deep layer finds

### Rohan (Anglo-Saxon Inspired)
- **Style**: Old English roots, simpler names
- **Materials**: Iron, leather, horsehair
- **Examples**: Herugrim (Théoden's sword), Gúthwinë (Éomer's sword)
- **Armor**: Mail coats, leather, ornate helms with horsehair crests
- **Naming pattern**: Compound words (Old English style)
- **Appropriate for**: Cavalry equipment, mid-layer finds

### Dale/Erebor (Dwarven Influence)
- **Style**: Practical, craft-focused
- **Materials**: Iron, steel, mithril
- **Examples**: Black Arrow (Bard's heirloom)
- **Armor**: Heavy mail, masks, functional helms
- **Naming pattern**: Descriptive, material-focused
- **Appropriate for**: Axes, hammers, heavy armor, deep layer finds

### Woodland Realm (Silvan Elves)
- **Style**: Nature-inspired, practical
- **Materials**: Wood, leather, light metals
- **Armor**: Lighter gear, cloaks, leather
- **Naming pattern**: Nature references (leaf, branch, star)
- **Appropriate for**: Bows, cloaks, light armor, early layer finds

### Dol Guldur (Corrupted/Shadow)
- **Style**: Dark, twisted, menacing
- **Materials**: Black iron, shadowy leather
- **Modifiers**: mor (dark), gûl (sorcery), dû (night)
- **Naming pattern**: [Dark adjective] + [Weapon]
- **Appropriate for**: Orcish gear, deep layer finds, corrupted items

## Tolkien Naming Patterns

### Two-Element Names
Most Tolkien weapon names have exactly two elements:
- Orcrist = Orc + crist (cleaver)
- Anglachel = Ang (iron) + lachel (flame)
- Gurthang = Gurth (death) + ang (iron)

### Naming by Function
- What it kills: Orcrist (Goblin-cleaver), Glamdring
- What it does: Angrist (Iron-cutter)
- How it looks: Ringil (Cold Star - glittered like ice)

### Material in Name
Common to include material:
- Ang- (iron): Anglachel, Angrist
- Mith- (grey/mithril): Mithril items

## Third Age Context (No First Age)

### AVOID
- Feanor, Feanorian (First Age)
- Gondolin (First Age city)
- Nargothrond (First Age)
- Beleriand (First Age lands)
- Morgoth (use "Enemy" or "Dark Lord")

### USE INSTEAD
- Númenórean heritage
- Gondorian style
- Rohirric
- Dwarven (Erebor/Khazad-dûm survivors)
- Silvan/Woodland
- Shadow of Dol Guldur

## Naming Proposal Framework

### By Depth/Layer

**Layer 1-2 (Forest Breach)**
- Silvan style: nature-inspired, light
- Examples: "Ranger's Blade", "Woodsman's Axe"

**Layer 3-4 (Orc Warrens)**
- Orcish/Dol Guldur style: crude, dark
- Examples: "Curved Orc-blade", "Shadow-forged Axe"

**Layer 5-6 (Torture Halls/Necropolis)**
- Corrupted style: death-themed, cold
- Examples: "Blade of the Pits", "Deathward Steel"

**Layer 7 (Inner Sanctum)**
- Ancient/Precious: Gondorian, mithril
- Examples: "Númenórean Longsword", "Mithril-bright Blade"
